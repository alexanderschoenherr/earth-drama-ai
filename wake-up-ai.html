Wake Up A call to stop sleepwalking through a world that profits from your numbness. Wake up. Look around. Look within. This is your life. Not a draft. Not a waiting room. Not a someday. This. Here. Now. You are breathing right now. Your heart is beating right now. The world is moving right now. And something is trying very hard to put you back to sleep. Do not let it. Do not scroll past your own life. Do not wait until the crisis is louder, the body is sicker, the world is crueler, the loneliness is deeper, or the truth is impossible to ignore. Wake up now. Not because everything is hopeless. Because everything is still possible. Not because you are broken. Because you are needed. Not tomorrow. Now. Come back now. Your life is not a rehearsal. Your body is not a costume. Your purpose is not hiding in some distant future where everything is easy and everyone finally understands you. You are here. You are alive. And something in this world is trying very hard to make you forget that. You Are Not Broken. You Are Buried. Most people are not asleep because they are stupid. They are asleep because they are tired. Buried under work. Buried under bills. Buried under shame. Buried under old pain. Buried under screens. Buried under fear. Buried under the version of themselves they built when they were just trying to survive. They laugh when they are not happy. They say they are fine when they are not fine. They stay busy so they do not have to feel. They stay distracted so they do not have to listen. They stay numb because being awake hurts at first. And I get it. This world is exhausting. It asks you to be productive when you are grieving. It asks you to smile when you are lonely. It asks you to brand yourself when you barely know yourself. It asks you to have an opinion on everything while barely giving you time to understand anything. It asks you to keep going, keep buying, keep scrolling, keep comparing, keep performing, keep pretending. And then it calls you lazy when your soul gets tired. No. You are not lazy. You are not crazy. You are not weak for feeling that something is wrong. Something is wrong. A lot is wrong. But that does not mean you are powerless. It means you have to wake up. Not in theory. Not as a vibe. Not as a quote you save and forget. In your actual life. Look Around Look around. No, really. Look. We are living in a world where pain becomes content. War becomes content. Loneliness becomes content. Outrage becomes content. Private grief becomes something to scroll past between ads. People are being trained to react instead of think. To compare instead of create. To perform instead of live. To numb instead of feel. To hate strangers instead of know themselves. This is not normal. We have been told to act like it is normal. It is not. The machines are getting smarter faster than we are becoming wiser. Politics has become theater while real people suffer. Cruelty gets rewarded with attention. Loneliness gets sold back to us as entertainment. Fear becomes strategy. Beauty gets filtered. Pain gets monetized. Your attention is not just your attention anymore. It is a business model. Your fear is not just your fear anymore. It is a business model. Your loneliness is not just your loneliness anymore. It is a business model. And if you are not awake, something else will live your life through you. An algorithm. A wound. A political tribe. A family script. A hunger for approval. A fear you inherited. A version of yourself you created to survive people who did not know how to love you. This is not a metaphor. This is happening. It is not normal to be this distracted. It is not normal to be this lonely. It is not normal to know more about strangers online than the people sitting next to us. It is not normal to be constantly stimulated and still feel empty. It is not normal to know what everyone is posting and not know what your own body is trying to tell you. It is not normal to be alive and barely feel here. So look around. Not to panic. Panic is not awakening. Panic is just fear running in circles. Look around so you can tell the truth. We are standing at a doorway. And unconsciousness is no longer harmless. A sleeping person can do damage. A sleeping species with powerful tools can damage everything. Maybe this is not the end of the world. But it is the end of sleepwalking as a safe way to live. Wake up. Look Within Now look within. Not to shame yourself. Not to punish yourself. Not to make a list of everything wrong with you. Look within because that is where your life has been trying to speak. Where have you gone numb? Where have you been pretending? Where have you been saying yes when your whole body means no? Where have you been calling fear “being realistic”? Where have you been calling avoidance “peace”? Where have you been calling comfort “healing”? Where have you been calling distraction “rest”? Where have you been shrinking so other people do not have to feel uncomfortable? Where have you been performing a version of yourself that gets accepted but does not feel alive? Be honest. Not cruel. Honest. There is a difference. Cruelty says, “You are worthless.” Honesty says, “You are disappearing, and it is time to come back.” Cruelty says, “You ruined everything.” Honesty says, “Something needs to change.” Cruelty says, “You should be ashamed.” Honesty says, “You are responsible for your life now.” That is the kind of truth we need. Not truth as a weapon. Truth as a doorway. I am not saying this because waking up is easy. I am saying it because staying asleep is costing us more than we know. Wake up from the screen. Wake up from the performance. Wake up from the inherited fear. Wake up from the lie that your life can wait. It cannot. This Is Your Life This is your life. Not the one you imagined. Not the one you lost. Not the one you compare to someone else’s. This one. With this body. This history. This pain. This longing. This strange little spark inside you that somehow has not gone out. That spark matters. Protect it. Feed it. Follow it. It is the part of you that still knows you were not born just to survive, consume, perform, and disappear. You were not born to become a more efficient ghost. You were not born to scroll through your own disappearance and call it rest. The point of life is not to win at sleepwalking. The point is to become fully alive — and help life become more alive around you. Love. Create. Tell the truth. Reduce suffering. Protect beauty. Stop passing your pain forward. Become conscious enough to love. Become honest enough to change. Become brave enough to do the right thing. Become present enough to stop abandoning yourself, other people, and the world. That does not mean you need to be famous. It does not mean you need to save the whole planet by yourself. It does not mean you need to be perfect, pure, enlightened, healed, impressive, successful, rich, or certain. It means you stop abandoning your part. Your part may be raising a child with tenderness. Your part may be making art. Your part may be telling the truth in your family. Your part may be healing the addiction. Your part may be leaving the relationship that is killing your spirit. Your part may be building something useful. Your part may be apologizing. Your part may be forgiving. Your part may be learning how to love without disappearing. Your part may be refusing to become cruel in a cruel world. Purpose is not always a lightning bolt. Sometimes purpose is the next honest thing. The next loving thing. The next brave thing. The next repair. The next creation. The next moment where you choose to be real instead of automatic. Wake up. Your life is not somewhere else. It is here. Come Back to Your Body Stop. Right now. Unclench your jaw. Drop your shoulders. Let your stomach soften. Breathe lower. Feel your feet. Notice the room. Notice the air. Notice your hands. Notice whether you are actually breathing or just surviving from the neck up. You are here. You are not a profile. You are not a résumé. You are not a brand. You are not a trauma response. You are not your worst mistake. You are not your family’s fear. You are not the opinion of people who never really saw you. You are not the mask you wore long enough to forget it was a mask. You are a living being. Start there. Your body is not separate from your awakening. Your body is where truth starts. It tells you when you are tense. It tells you when you are afraid. It tells you when you are lying. It tells you when you are pretending. It tells you when something is wrong. It tells you when something is alive. And many of us have been trained to leave it. We live in our screens. We live in our thoughts. We live in our image. We live in the future. We live in old shame. We live in what other people might think. We are not here. And the world needs people who are here. So come back. To your breath. To your hands. To your heart. To the people in front of you. To the work that is yours. To the truth you already know but keep avoiding. Come back. This cannot stay theoretical anymore. Not if you want to live. Not just exist. Live. The Assignment Waking up is not a mood. It is a responsibility. It is not reading something inspiring and then going back to the same unconscious life. Waking up means your life starts changing. You tell the truth more often. You lie to yourself less. You pay attention to what you consume. You stop feeding every appetite that weakens you. You stop calling avoidance “self-care.” You stop confusing cynicism with intelligence. You stop letting the world make you cruel. And then you do the right thing. Not always the easy thing. Not always the thing that gets applause. The right thing. Protect your attention. Take care of your body. Tell the truth. Repair what you can. Make something real. Love the people in front of you better. Refuse cruelty. Refuse despair. Refuse the lie that your life does not matter. Do not just complain about the world becoming empty. Become full. Do not just complain about the world becoming cruel. Become brave and kind. Do not just complain about the world becoming fake. Become real. You do not need the whole map. You do not need to know your life purpose in perfect language. You do not need to solve every wound before you start living. You do not need to wait until you feel ready. Most people are waiting for clarity before they move. But sometimes clarity comes from movement. So begin. Begin with the room you are in. Begin with your body. Begin with your next choice. Begin with one honest conversation. Begin with one act of discipline. Begin with one thing you stop doing because it makes you smaller. Begin with one thing you start doing because it makes you more alive. I am not asking you to become fearless. I am asking you to stop letting fear make every decision. Wake up does not mean become someone else. It means stop betraying who you already are. You do not have to save everything. But you do have to stop abandoning your part of everything. That is enough to begin. And if enough people begin, the world changes. Not all at once. Not magically. Not perfectly. But truly. One awake person becomes a signal. Two awake people become a relationship. A group of awake people becomes a culture. A culture of awake people becomes a future. Come Back I know you are tired. I know the world is heavy. I know waking up hurts at first. I know it can feel easier to stay numb, stay busy, stay entertained, stay angry, stay ironic, stay hidden, stay safe. But your numbness will not protect you. Your performance will not fulfill you. Your distractions will not love you back. Your fear will not become wisdom just because you obey it long enough. Come back. Look around. Look within. This is your life. Not someday. Not later. Now. This breath. This body. This chance. This doorway. Wake up. Not because everything is hopeless. Because everything is still possible. Wake up because love needs hands. Wake up because truth needs voices. Wake up because beauty needs makers. Wake up because the future needs people who are actually here. Wake up because you are needed. Wake up because this is it. Not the end. The doorway. Stand up inside your life. Walk through.
